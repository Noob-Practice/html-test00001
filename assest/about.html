We are a educational institution that operates independently of government funding and control. These schools are typically funded through tuition fees, donations, and private grants, which allows them to offer a curriculum and set of policies that may differ from public schools. Private schools often have smaller class sizes, which can result in more personalized attention for students and closer teacher-student relationships. Many private schools emphasize academic excellence and may offer specialized programs such as advanced placement (AP) courses, International Baccalaureate (IB) programs, or focus on specific educational philosophies like Montessori or Waldorf. Extracurricular activities, including sports, arts, and music programs, are usually a key part of their offerings, helping to promote a well-rounded education. Private schools can be secular or religiously affiliated, with the latter incorporating faith-based teachings into their curriculum. Admission processes often involve entrance exams, interviews, and evaluations of previous academic performance, making them more selective than public schools. While they tend to offer a high standard of education, the cost can be prohibitive for some families, which is why many private schools offer financial aid or scholarships to help students from diverse backgrounds.
